# 📖 Slack: Product-Led Growth (PLG)

## 📋 Summary Table
| Metric | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Industry** | B2B SaaS / Collaboration |
| **Stage** | Hypergrowth |
| **Key Move** | Using a "Bottom-Up" adoption model to bypass traditional IT procurement. |
| **Impact** | Fastest-growing B2B SaaS company in history (at the time). |

---

## 🚀 The Backstory
Slack was famously born out of a failed gaming company (Tiny Speck). They built a tool for internal use that was so good they pivoted the entire company to it.

## ⚠️ The Central Problem
Traditional enterprise software was sold "Top-Down" to CIOs. This led to high sales costs and low user engagement. Slack needed to prove that a communication tool could spread organically.

## 🛠️ The Solution & Execution
1. **The "Magic Number"**: Slack discovered that once a team sent 2,000 messages, they were 93% likely to stick. The PM team focused entirely on getting teams to that number.
2. **Integrations as a Moat**: By making Slack the "hub" for Jira, GitHub, and Google Drive, it became impossible to leave.
3. **Fair Billing Policy**: Only charging for active users—a massive differentiator in B2B SaaS.

## 📉 Key Metrics & Results
- **Conversion**: Extremely high free-to-paid conversion rates due to the 10k message limit.
- **D30 Retention**: Industry-leading retention for teams that reached the "Magic Number."

## 💡 Key Lessons for PMs
1. **Find your "Aha! Moment"**: Identify the specific usage threshold that correlates with long-term retention.
2. **Reduce Onboarding Friction**: Slack’s "Slackbot" made the initial setup feel like a conversation, not a configuration.
