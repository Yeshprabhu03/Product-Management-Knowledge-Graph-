# 🛠️ Amazon Working Backwards (PR/FAQ)

## 📖 Definition
Working Backwards is a product development process pioneered by Amazon. It starts with the customer and the end experience, and works backwards until the team arrives at the minimum set of requirements.

## 🎯 When Would I Use This?
1. **New Product Proposals**: When trying to get buy-in for a major new initiative.
2. **Customer-Centric Alignment**: When the team is getting lost in technical constraints and needs to refocus on user value.
3. **Clarifying Vision**: To ensure everyone has a shared, vivid understanding of what success looks like.

## 🏗️ How to Apply It
1. **The Press Release (PR)**: Write a one-page announcement of the product launch. Use customer-centric language. What problem does it solve? Why is it exciting?
2. **The FAQ**: Write a document answering potential questions from customers (External FAQ) and stakeholders (Internal FAQ). Include the tough questions about cost, privacy, and technical hurdles.
3. **Visuals**: Create simple mockups of the customer experience.

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls
- **Being Too Technical**: The PR should be readable by a layperson. Save the jargon for the Tech Spec.
- **Ignoring the "Internal" FAQ**: The internal questions are where you address feasibility and business model.
