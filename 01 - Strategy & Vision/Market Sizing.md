# 📊 Market Sizing

## 📖 Definition
Market Sizing is the process of estimating the potential revenue and user base available for a product. It is traditionally broken down into three layers: **TAM** (Total Addressable Market — the total market demand if there were no competition), **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market — the portion of the TAM targeted by your products), and **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market — the portion of the SAM that you can realistically capture in the short term). A "Bottom-up" analysis (Unit Economics × Target Users) is generally considered more credible for product planning than a "Top-down" analysis (Percentage of a massive industry report).

## 🎯 Why It Matters for PMs
Product Managers use market sizing to:
1. **Justify Investment**: Proving to leadership that the opportunity is large enough to warrant the cost of development.
2. **Focus GTM Efforts**: Identifying which segments (SOM) to target first for the fastest growth.
3. **Set Realistic Goals**: Ensuring that OKRs and revenue targets are grounded in the actual number of potential customers.

## 🏗️ How to Apply It
1. **Define Your Target Profile**: Clearly outline the characteristics of the "Ideal Customer Profile" (ICP) for your product.
2. **Perform Bottom-up Analysis**: Calculate the market size by taking the number of potential customers in your ICP and multiplying it by your expected Annual Contract Value (ACV).
3. **Determine SAM and SOM**: Filter the TAM by geography, industry, and your own technical/sales constraints to find your realistic short-term targets.
4. **Validate with Top-down Data**: Use industry reports only to "sanity check" your bottom-up numbers, ensuring they aren't wildly unrealistic.
5. **Update with Actuals**: Refine your sizing model as you gain real data on customer acquisition costs and actual contract values.

## 📦 Real-World Example
> **Zoom — 2019 vs 2021**
> Pre-COVID, Zoom's **TAM** was essentially all businesses globally that needed video meetings. Their **SAM** was SMBs and enterprises with distributed teams, and their **SOM** was those they could realistically capture from incumbents like Webex. However, COVID-19 expanded their TAM overnight to include almost every individual on earth (education, social, fitness). Their market sizing model had to be completely rebuilt in 2020 to account for this fundamental shift in human behavior.
> *PM Lesson: Market size is not static; external shocks or technology shifts can expand or contract your TAM in a matter of months.*

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls
- **Over-optimistic SOM**: Assuming you can capture 10% of a massive market without a clear sales or distribution plan.
- **Ignoring Competition**: Failing to account for how much of the market is already "locked in" to rivals with high switching costs.
- **Top-down Delusion**: Basing an entire business case on "capturing 1% of the $500B healthcare market" without defining which specific users in which specific segment will actually pay.
