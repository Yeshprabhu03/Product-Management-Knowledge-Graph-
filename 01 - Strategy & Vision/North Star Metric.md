# 🌟 North Star Metric

## 📖 Definition
The North Star Metric (NSM) is the single key figure that best captures the core value your product delivers to its customers. It is meant to be a leading indicator of long-term business success, rather than a lagging indicator like revenue or a vanity metric like "total registered users." A well-chosen NSM aligns the entire product team around a shared goal that directly correlates with user satisfaction and retention.

## 🎯 Why It Matters for PMs
For a Product Manager, the North Star Metric is critical because:
1. **Simplifies Alignment**: Provides a single point of focus that cuts through the noise of competing priorities.
2. **Measures Value, Not Output**: Shifts the team's focus from "shipping features" to "delivering outcomes."
3. **Predicts Growth**: Helps in identifying whether the product is actually gaining traction in a way that will lead to sustainable revenue.

## 🏗️ How to Apply It
1. **Identify the Core Value**: Determine the exact moment or action when a user receives the primary benefit of your product.
2. **Draft Potential Metrics**: Brainstorm figures that track that value (e.g., "Nights Booked," "Messages Sent," "Workouts Completed").
3. **Test for Leading Indicator Qualities**: Ensure the metric predicts future retention and revenue, rather than just reflecting past success.
4. **Communicate and Cascade**: Share the NSM with the organization and help sub-teams identify their own "input metrics" that drive the North Star.
5. **Monitor and Refine**: Check for unintended consequences (e.g., optimizing for messages sent leading to spam) and adjust the metric as the product evolves.

## 📦 Real-World Example
> **Airbnb — 2013**
> Airbnb's North Star Metric is "Nights Booked." This metric perfectly encapsulates the value for both guests (finding a place to stay) and hosts (making money). It is a leading indicator of revenue and platform growth. By focusing on increasing the number of nights booked, the team was able to prioritize features like professional photography and "Instant Book" that directly removed friction from the booking process.
> *PM Lesson: A great North Star Metric should reflect the core transaction that satisfies both sides of your marketplace or ecosystem.*

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls
- **Choosing a Vanity Metric**: Metrics like "Page Views" or "Registered Users" can go up without actually delivering more value to customers.
- **Ignoring the Revenue Link**: While NSM shouldn't be revenue, it must have a clear and proven relationship to how the business makes money.
- **Setting and Forgetting**: Failing to re-evaluate the NSM as the product reaches different stages of maturity or enters new markets.
