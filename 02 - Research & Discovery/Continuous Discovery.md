# 🔄 Continuous Discovery

## 📖 Definition
Continuous Discovery is a product management framework, popularized by Teresa Torres, that emphasizes making discovery a weekly habit rather than a one-time "phase" at the beginning of a project. It involves the "Product Trio" (PM, Designer, and Lead Engineer) having at least one touchpoint with a customer every week. The core artifact of this process is the **Opportunity Solution Tree**, which helps the team map desired business outcomes to specific user opportunities, solutions, and experiments.

## 🎯 Why It Matters for PMs
For a Product Manager, continuous discovery is essential to:
1. **Avoid "Big Batch" Failure**: Breaking research into small, manageable chunks that inform the team's decisions in real-time.
2. **Build a "Truth-Seeking" Culture**: Ensuring the team is always grounded in the current reality of the user's life rather than their own assumptions.
3. **Identify Micro-Opportunities**: Catching small but high-impact user needs that would be missed in a large, formal research project.

## 🏗️ How to Apply It
1. **Assemble the Product Trio**: Ensure the PM, Designer, and Engineer are all involved in at least some part of the weekly discovery calls.
2. **Schedule Recurring Calls**: Automate the recruitment process so that at least one 20-minute user call appears on the trio's calendar every week.
3. **Map the Opportunity Solution Tree**: Start with a clear outcome (e.g., "reduce churn"), map the user pains (opportunities), brainstorm solutions, and design small experiments.
4. **Conduct "Snapshot" Interviews**: Focus each weekly call on a specific story or experience the user had recently, rather than broad hypothetical questions.
5. **Update the Tree and Roadmap**: Use the insights from every call to immediately prune or expand your Opportunity Solution Tree and adjust your tactical priorities.

## 📦 Real-World Example
> **A Growth-Stage Fintech — 2022**
> A PM at a fintech startup built weekly 20-minute user calls into the team's regular sprint cadence. During one of these "routine" calls, they discovered that users were frequently taking screenshots of their monthly reports to share with friends via WhatsApp because there was no native share feature. The team built a simple "Share to WhatsApp" button in 2 weeks, which led to a 40% increase in team-based referrals. This insight was only possible because of the continuous, low-stakes touchpoints with users.
> *PM Lesson: Continuous discovery allows you to catch the "small" user behaviors that reveal "large" product opportunities.*

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls
- **Treating Discovery as a "Phase"**: Stopping user calls once the "requirements" are written or when the team enters a "crunch period."
- **Excluding Engineering and Design**: Having the PM do all the discovery in isolation, which leads to a lack of shared understanding and buy-in.
- **Confirmation Bias**: Using the weekly calls only to find "proof" for the features you already want to build, rather than searching for new opportunities.
