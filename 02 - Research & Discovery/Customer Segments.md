# 📑 Customer Segments

## 📖 Definition
Customer Segmentation is the practice of dividing a product's user base into groups of individuals who share similar characteristics, behaviors, or needs. While demographic segmentation (age, location) is common, **Behavioral Segmentation** (power users vs. casual users vs. at-risk users) is generally the most actionable for product managers. Effective segmentation allows teams to tailor product experiences, marketing messages, and support playbooks to the specific needs of each group.

## 🎯 Why It Matters for PMs
For a Product Manager, segmentation is crucial because:
1. **Identifies Churn Drivers**: Understanding why certain segments are leaving while others are staying allows for targeted retention strategies.
2. **Optimizes GTM Efforts**: Helping marketing and sales focus on the segments with the highest Lifetime Value (LTV).
3. **Informs Feature Customization**: Allowing the product to offer different workflows or onboarding paths based on the user's specific context (e.g., enterprise vs. individual).

## 🏗️ How to Apply It
1. **Collect Behavioral Data**: Use analytics tools to track how different users interact with your core features.
2. **Define Segment Criteria**: Choose the variables that most impact your product's success (e.g., company size, industry, or usage frequency).
3. **Analyze Segment Health**: Measure retention, conversion, and NPS separately for each segment to identify which are "thriving" and which are "struggling."
4. **Develop Targeted Playbooks**: Create specific product improvements or communication strategies for your most important segments.
5. **Prune and Refine**: Regularly re-evaluate segments to ensure they are still meaningful and that users aren't shifting between groups in unexpected ways.

## 📦 Real-World Example
> **Zoom — 2020**
> Zoom's segments include Consumers, SMBs, Enterprise, and Education. While the core product is the same, each segment has entirely different drivers and pain points. For example, Consumers often churn to free tiers if they don't see the value in pro features, while Enterprise customers churn primarily over security and compliance gaps. By segmenting their retention playbooks, Zoom was able to maintain high growth across all groups simultaneously.
> *PM Lesson: Treating every user the same is a recipe for high churn; different segments require different definitions of "success" and "value."*

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls
- **Over-Segmenting**: Creating so many small groups that the team cannot possibly build or market to all of them effectively.
- **Demographic-Only Bias**: Relying on age or location when the user's actual behavior within the app is a much stronger predictor of their needs.
- **Ignoring the "Non-User" Segment**: Failing to analyze the people who visit your site but never convert, which is a key segment for acquisition growth.
