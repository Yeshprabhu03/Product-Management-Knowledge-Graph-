# Surveys

## Definition
Surveys are a quantitative research method used to gather structured data from a large group of users. They are essential for validating qualitative findings from user interviews at scale. Key product surveys include the **Product-Market Fit (PMF) Survey** (developed by Sean Ellis), **Net Promoter Score (NPS)**, **Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)**, and **Customer Effort Score (CES)**. A successful survey requires careful question design to avoid bias and a clear plan for how the resulting data will be used to make product decisions.

## Why It Matters for PMs
For a Product Manager, surveys provide:
1. **Scale and Confidence**: Proving that an insight found in a few interviews is representative of the broader user base.
2. **Benchmarking**: Tracking changes in user sentiment over time and comparing your product's performance to industry standards.
3. **Segment Analysis**: Identifying which specific groups of users are most satisfied or most at-risk of churning.

## How to Apply It
1. **Identify the Goal**: Determine if you are measuring general satisfaction (NPS), task-specific ease (CES), or overall product-market fit.
2. **Design Bias-Free Questions**: Use neutral language and ensure your response scales are consistent and balanced.
3. **Select the Right Sample**: Ensure you are surveying the right users at the right time (e.g., don't ask for a review immediately after a bug report).
4. **Analyze Quantitative and Qualitative Data**: Look at the scores, but pay close attention to the open-ended text responses for context.
5. **Close the Feedback Loop**: Share the results with the team and communicate back to the users how their feedback is being used to improve the product.

## Real-World Example
> **Superhuman — 2017**
> Superhuman famously used the Sean Ellis PMF survey to find their product-market fit. They asked users: "How would you feel if you could no longer use Superhuman?" They found that while their overall score was below the 40% "very disappointed" threshold, the score was much higher among startup founders and power email users. They decided to "fire" all other segments and focus exclusively on these power users, which allowed them to double their NPS and significantly reduce churn.
> *PM Lesson: Surveys are not just for measuring satisfaction; they are powerful tools for identifying your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP).*

## Common Pitfalls
- **Leading Questions**: Phrasing questions in a way that encourages a specific answer (e.g., "How much did you enjoy our new feature?").
- **Survey Fatigue**: Sending too many surveys to the same users, which leads to lower response rates and lower-quality data.
- **Ignoring the Open-Ended Responses**: Focusing only on the numerical scores while missing the critical "why" hidden in the text comments.
