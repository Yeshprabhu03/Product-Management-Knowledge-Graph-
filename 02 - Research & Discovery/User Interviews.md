# 🗣️ User Interviews

## 📖 Definition
User Interviews are one-on-one qualitative conversations between a researcher (often the PM) and a user or potential customer. They are designed to uncover deep insights into user behavior, motivations, and pain points. Interviews are typically categorized as **Problem Interviews** (exploring the problem space before a solution exists) or **Solution Interviews** (validating a specific prototype or feature). A key principle for PMs is "The Mom Test": never pitch your idea; instead, ask about the user's past behavior to avoid receiving biased or polite feedback.

## 🎯 Why It Matters for PMs
For a Product Manager, user interviews are the best way to:
1. **Uncover the "Why"**: Getting behind the quantitative data to understand the underlying motivations for user actions.
2. **Build Empathy**: Hearing the user's struggle in their own words, which helps in advocating for them during internal discussions.
3. **Identify New Opportunities**: Discovering pain points and workflows that the team hadn't even considered yet.

## 🏗️ How to Apply It
1. **Define the Learning Objective**: Clearly state what one or two things you want to learn from this set of interviews (e.g., "how do users currently manage their task lists?").
2. **Recruit Representative Users**: Find people who match your target persona or segment, ensuring a mix of power users and new users.
3. **Draft an Open-Ended Script**: Write questions that encourage storytelling (e.g., "Tell me about the last time you tried to...") and avoid "yes/no" or hypothetical questions.
4. **Conduct the Interview**: Listen more than you speak. Use "the five whys" to dig deeper into the user's answers. Record and transcribe the sessions.
5. **Synthesize and Share**: Identify recurring themes and "aha" moments across all interviews and share the raw clips or key insights with the broader team.

## 📦 Real-World Example
> **Intercom — 2012**
> In its early days, Intercom's founders conducted dozens of user interviews to understand how people were using their new messaging tool. They discovered that users didn't actually want a "support ticket system" — they wanted to feel heard and get help quickly. This qualitative insight reshaped the entire product strategy from being a ticket-management tool to a messenger-first platform, which became their core competitive differentiator.
> *PM Lesson: Quantitative data tells you what is happening, but only user interviews can tell you why it's happening.*

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls
- **Pitching Your Solution**: Turning the interview into a sales pitch, which causes the user to give polite, useless feedback.
- **Asking Hypothetical Questions**: "Would you use X?" is a dangerous question because people are notoriously bad at predicting their own future behavior.
- **Ignoring the Negative**: Only listening to the feedback that confirms your existing beliefs while dismissing the users who struggle or dislike the product.
