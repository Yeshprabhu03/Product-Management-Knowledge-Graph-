# 📖 Airbnb: Solving the Trust Problem

## 📋 Summary Table
| Metric | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Industry** | Marketplace / Travel |
| **Stage** | Early-stage survival |
| **Key Move** | Using high-quality photography to overcome the "Stranger Danger" trust barrier. |
| **Impact** | Doubled revenue in NYC within weeks; proved the design-led growth hypothesis. |

---

## 🚀 The Backstory
In 2009, Airbnb was failing. They were making only $200 a week and were on the verge of shutting down.

## ⚠️ The Central Problem
The "Trust Barrier." Users were afraid to stay in a stranger's house because the photos were blurry and uninviting. The product looked "sketchy."

## 🛠️ The Solution & Execution
1. **The Photography Experiment**: Joe Gebbia and Brian Chesky went to NYC themselves, rented a camera, and took professional photos of listings.
2. **Design-Led Growth**: They realized that in a marketplace of strangers, "Trust is the currency."
3. **Reviews & Verification**: Building a robust two-way review system to reinforce trust.

## 📉 Key Metrics & Results
- **Revenue**: Weekly revenue doubled in NYC immediately after the new photos went live.
- **Scale**: This "unscalable" experiment became the blueprint for their global photography program.

## 💡 Key Lessons for PMs
1. **Do things that don't scale**: Sometimes the solution isn't code; it's a manual intervention that proves a hypothesis.
2. **Design is not just "look and feel"**: It's about solving a fundamental psychological barrier (Trust).
