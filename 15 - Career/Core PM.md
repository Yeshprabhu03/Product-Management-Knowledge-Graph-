# Core PM

## 📖 Definition
The Core PM (or Generalist PM) is the foundational archetype of product management. They own the "End-to-End" user experience of a specific feature set or product module. Unlike specialists who focus on a single lever (like growth or data), the Core PM must balance user research, UI/UX design, business logic, and engineering constraints to ship value. They are the "Minis-CEOs" of their domain, responsible for discovering what to build and ensuring it is built correctly.

## 🎯 Why It Matters
The Core PM role is the primary entry point for most PM careers and the backbone of most product organizations.
1. **Holistic Ownership**: They are responsible for the "Outcome," not just a specific metric.
2. **Translation Layer**: They are the primary bridge between the "Problem Space" (Users) and the "Solution Space" (Engineering).
3. **Versatility**: A strong Core PM can transition into any specialization because they master the fundamentals of discovery, prioritization, and execution.

## 🏗️ How to Apply It (The Core PM Toolkit)
1. **Problem Discovery**: Conduct user interviews and surveys to identify the "Job to be Done."
2. **Requirement Definition**: Write high-fidelity PRDs that define the *What* and *Why* without over-prescribing the *How*.
3. **Stakeholder Management**: Align Design, Eng, and Marketing on the roadmap.
4. **Execution**: Manage the backlog, run sprints, and define acceptance criteria for every ticket.
5. **Impact Measurement**: Use qualitative and quantitative data to determine if the launch solved the original user problem.

## 📦 Real-World Example
> **Airbnb — The "Search & Discovery" PM — 2018**
> A Core PM at Airbnb owned the "Search Results" page. Their job wasn't just to make search faster (Technical PM) or increase booking conversion (Growth PM). They had to deeply understand the *psychology* of a traveler. They conducted research that revealed users felt "overwhelmed" by choices. The Core PM then collaborated with Design to implement "Airbnb Plus" and "Categories," shifting the experience from a list of results to a curated travel guide. This required balancing model accuracy (Eng), visual aesthetics (Design), and trust (Legal).
> *PM Lesson: Core PM success is measured by the quality of the user's progress, not the optimization of a single funnel step.*

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls
- **The "Project Manager" Trap**: Focusing 100% on delivery (schedules and tickets) while ignoring the discovery of the "Next Big Thing."
- **Lack of Vision**: Shipping a series of disconnected "features" that don't add up to a coherent product story.
- **Over-specialization early**: Trying to act like an AI PM when the core problem is a simple UI friction issue.
