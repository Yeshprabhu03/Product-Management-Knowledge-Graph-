# 🗺️ Product Leadership MOC

## 📖 Definition
The Product Leadership Map of Content (MOC) is the central hub for all topics related to the growth, management, and strategic influence of a Product Manager. It connects the tactical skills of "Career Management" with the philosophical skills of "Product Leadership." It serves as a guide for PMs moving from "Feature Owners" to "Business Leaders" who can shape the future of their industry and their teams.

## 🎯 Core Pillars
1. **The Career Track**:
    - [[15 - Career & Leadership/PM Career Ladder]] — Navigating the path from APM to CPO.
    - [[15 - Career & Leadership/Interviewing for AI PM Roles]] — Mastering the specialized AI PM hiring process.
2. **The Leadership Track**:
    - [[15 - Career & Leadership/Leadership vs Management]] — Balancing execution with vision and influence.
    - [[12 - Leadership & Stakeholders/Managing Up]] — Strategically influencing your manager and executives.
3. **The External Brand**:
    - [[15 - Career & Leadership/Building a Personal Brand]] — Developing your industry authority and niche.

## 🏗️ How to Use This MOC
- **For Junior PMs**: Focus on the **Career Ladder** and **Interviewing** notes to build your foundational path.
- **For Senior PMs**: Focus on **Leadership vs Management** and **Managing Up** to transition into high-impact roles.
- **For Aspiring Leaders**: Focus on **Personal Brand** to build the external credibility needed for executive positions.

## 📦 Real-World Example
> **The "Career Pivot" — 2023**
> A Senior PM at a legacy SaaS company used this framework to pivot into a Lead AI PM role. They used the **Interviewing for AI PM** note to master the technical cases, the **Personal Brand** note to start writing about "AI in FinTech," and the **Leadership vs Management** note to convince their CPO that they could lead the company's new AI task force. By treating their career as a "Product," they were able to double their impact and salary in under 12 months.
> *PM Lesson: Your career is the most important product you will ever manage; apply the same rigor to your own growth as you do to your roadmap.*
