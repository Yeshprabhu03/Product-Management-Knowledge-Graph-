# 🎤 Interviewing for AI PM Roles

## 📖 Definition
Interviewing for AI PM Roles is the specialized process of evaluating a candidate's ability to build and scale machine learning products. Unlike traditional PM interviews (which focus on product sense and execution), AI PM interviews add a **Technical/Probabilistic Layer**. Candidates are tested on their understanding of **Data Strategy**, **Model Evaluation**, **Inference Constraints**, and **AI Ethics**. The goal is to find PMs who can navigate the "uncertainty" of research while delivering "deterministic" product value.

## 🎯 Why It Matters for PMs
For a candidate, mastering the AI PM interview is the "Gatekeeper" to the most high-leverage roles in tech. It matters because:
1. **Signals "AI Literacy"**: Proving you can talk to engineers about "embeddings" and "fine-tuning" without being a researcher yourself.
2. **Evaluates Risk Management**: Demonstrating how you would handle an AI that is "90% accurate" but fails in a high-stakes way.
3. **Assesses Business Logic**: Showing you can balance the massive costs of AI with a sustainable business model and pricing strategy.

## 🏗️ How to Apply It
1. **Master the "AI Case Study"**: Practice explaining how you would build an AI feature from scratch, from data collection to model selection to guardrails.
2. **Be Prepared for "System Design" for PMs**: Know how to draw a high-level architecture of a RAG system or an AI agent workflow.
3. **Quantify Your AI Impact**: Use specific metrics (e.g., "Reduced hallucination rate by 15%" or "Lowered inference cost by 40%") to prove your effectiveness.
4. **Demonstrate "Ethical Reasoning"**: Be ready to discuss how you identify and mitigate bias in training data or how you would comply with the EU AI Act.
5. **Ask "Technical Depth" Questions**: Show your own expertise by asking the interviewer about their "Data Flywheel," their "Model Retraining cadence," or their "GPU orchestration."

## 📦 Real-World Example
> **The "Hypothetical Hallucination" Question — 2024**
> A common AI PM interview question at top labs like OpenAI or Anthropic is: *"You are building a medical AI that is 99% accurate, but the 1% failure could be fatal. How do you design the product?"* A successful candidate doesn't just talk about "making the model better." They talk about **Human-in-the-Loop UX**, **Confidence Indicators**, **Transparency**, and **Legal Fallbacks**. They prove they can build a "Safe System" around an "Imperfect Model."
> *PM Lesson: In an AI interview, the "correct" answer is often about how you manage the AI's "inevitable mistakes."*

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls
- **Being "Too Technical"**: Trying to sound like a researcher and getting bogged down in "loss functions" while ignoring the "User Problem."
- **Being "Not Technical Enough"**: Hand-waving the "how" of the AI (e.g., "the AI will just know what to do") without understanding the underlying data or model constraints.
- **Ignoring the "Cost" side**: Proposing a massive, expensive model for a low-value feature, which shows a lack of "Commercial Acumen."
