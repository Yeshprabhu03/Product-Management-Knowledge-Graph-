# Growth PM

## 📖 Definition
A Growth PM is an archetype focused on the "Scientific Optimization" of the user journey. Unlike a Core PM who builds new value, the Growth PM focuses on **removing friction** and **surfacing existing value** to more people. They operate primarily on the "AARRR" funnel (Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Referral, Revenue). Their primary tools are A/B testing, rapid experimentation, and data analysis.

## 🎯 Why It Matters
A great product that nobody uses is a failure. The Growth PM ensures the product's value reaches its maximum potential audience.
1. **Velocity**: They run 10x more experiments than any other PM archetype.
2. **Precision**: They find the "Inflection Points" in the data where a small change (e.g., a better onboarding flow) leads to a massive compounding result.
3. **Loop Thinking**: They build "Growth Loops" where the use of the product by one user naturally leads to the acquisition of another.

## 🏗️ How to Apply It
1. **Funnel Analysis**: Identify exactly where users are dropping off (e.g., "70% of users leave at the credit card screen").
2. **Hypothesis Generation**: Formulate clear "If [Change], then [Metric] will increase because [Psychological Reason]" statements.
3. **Experimentation (A/B Testing)**: Run controlled tests to validate hypotheses.
4. **Onboarding Optimization**: Relentlessly focus on the "Time to Aha!" — the seconds it takes for a new user to realize the product's value.
5. **Pricing & Packaging**: Test different price points and feature bundles to maximize LTV (Lifetime Value).

## 📦 Real-World Example
> **Duolingo — The "Notification" Growth PM — 2021**
> The Growth PM team at Duolingo noticed that retention was the primary driver of their long-term growth. Instead of building new language courses (Core PM), they experimented with the "Duo" owl notifications. They found that changing the copy from "Time to study!" to "Duo is sad you missed your lesson" (the guilt-trip strategy) increased daily active users (DAU) by over 5%. They then ran a "Bandit" experiment to determine the optimal time of day for each individual user to receive the ping.
> *PM Lesson: Growth PM is a game of marginal gains. 5% here and 3% there compound into 2x growth over a year.*

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls
- **"Dark Patterns"**: Using deceptive UX to juice short-term metrics (like "trick" unsubscribe buttons) that destroy long-term brand trust.
- **Local Maxima**: Optimizing a single button so much that you miss the fact that the entire feature is no longer relevant.
- **Ignoring Retention**: Focusing entirely on "Acquisition" (new users) while the "Bucket is Leaking" (old users are leaving).
