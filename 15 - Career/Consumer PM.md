# Consumer PM

## 📖 Definition
A Consumer PM (B2C PM) is an archetype that focuses on building products for the "End User" — the person who uses the product in their daily personal life. This role is defined by **High Volume, Rapid Feedback, and Behavioral Psychology**. Unlike B2B, where decisions are often "Rational" and "Multi-stakeholder," B2C decisions are often "Emotional," "Impulsive," and "Solo." Consumer PMs live in the world of mobile apps, social media, entertainment, and e-commerce.

## 🎯 Why It Matters
Consumer products are the most competitive and volatile part of the tech ecosystem.
1. **The "Engagement" Economy**: They must fight for "Attention" against every other app on the user's phone.
2. **Viral Potential**: A Consumer PM can see their product go from 1,000 to 1,000,000 users in a week if they hit the right psychological trigger.
3. **Implicit Feedback**: Because they have millions of users, they can use A/B testing to "Hear" what users want through their actions, even when the users can't articulate it.

## 🏗️ How to Apply It
1. **Behavioral Psychology**: Use frameworks like the "Hook Model" (Trigger, Action, Variable Reward, Investment) to build habits.
2. **UI/UX Obsession**: In B2C, a "Confusing Button" is a "Lost User." Relentlessly simplify the interface.
3. **Community & Network Effects**: Build features that become more valuable as more of the user's friends join.
4. **Emotional Discovery**: Go beyond "Does it work?" to "How does it make them feel?" Use sentiment analysis and ethnographic research.

## 📦 Real-World Example
> **TikTok — The "For You" Feed PM — 2018**
> The Consumer PM for TikTok's feed realized that the "Barrier to Value" on Instagram (having to follow people) was too high for new users. They designed the "For You" feed to be entirely passive: you don't follow anyone; you just watch. The PM's success was measured by **"Time Spent"** and **"Session Frequency."** They collaborated with AI PMs to ensure the recommendation model reacted to "Micro-signals" (like re-watching a 3-second loop) to immediately pivot the content.
> *PM Lesson: Consumer PMs solve for 'Dopamine' and 'Ease of Use' above all else.*

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls
- **The "N=1" Trap**: Thinking you are the target user and building features that *you* want, while ignoring the data of the other 10 million users.
- **Optimizing for "Crap"**: Driving high engagement through controversial or harmful content that eventually leads to user burnout and regulatory scrutiny.
- **Ignoring Monetization**: Building a massive, "Fun" product that has no clear path to making money.
