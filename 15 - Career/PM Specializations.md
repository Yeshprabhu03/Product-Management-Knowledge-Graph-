# PM Specializations

## Definition
Product Management has evolved into distinct specializations, each requiring a different primary skill set and operating in a different part of the product ecosystem. The main archetypes are: Core/General PM, Technical PM, Growth PM, Data PM, AI/ML PM, Platform PM, Consumer PM, and Enterprise/B2B PM. Understanding which track you are on — or want to move to — shapes what skills to develop, how to read job descriptions, and which companies to target.

## Why It Matters for PMs
1. **Interview Prep**: Each specialization has different interview formats — Growth PMs face SQL and funnel questions; AI PMs face model evaluation and trade-off scenarios; Platform PMs face API design questions.
2. **Skill Investment**: A Core PM spending time on ML infrastructure internals has misallocated their development time; an AI PM who cannot read a confusion matrix is under-equipped for their role.
3. **Team Dynamics**: Knowing the specialization of your PM peers prevents scope conflicts and clarifies who owns which decisions.

## How to Apply It
1. **Audit Your Current Role**: List the top 5 decisions you made last quarter — which archetype do they most resemble?
2. **Identify Your Target**: Decide which specialization aligns with your domain interest and career goals.
3. **Find the Skill Gap**: Each archetype has a defining hard skill — identify yours and build a 90-day learning plan.
4. **Target the Right Companies**: Consumer PM roles concentrate at B2C companies (Meta, Spotify, Airbnb); Enterprise PM roles at B2B SaaS (Salesforce, Workday, ServiceNow); AI PM roles at AI-native companies (Anthropic, Cohere, Glean) and AI-integrated incumbents.
5. **Signal the Specialization**: Tailor your resume and portfolio to show the specific metrics and decisions that matter for each archetype — a Growth PM resume leads with activation and retention numbers; an AI PM resume leads with model trade-off decisions.

## Real-World Example
> **Stripe — 2022**
> Stripe's PM org illustrates how specializations co-exist on one platform. Their API PM team (Platform PMs) owns developer experience and SDKs; their Radar PM team (AI/ML PMs) owns fraud model accuracy and false-positive rates; their Billing PM team (Core PMs) owns revenue recognition flows. Each PM archetype operates under a completely different success metric: API PM = developer time-to-first call; Radar PM = fraud loss rate vs. false-positive rate; Billing PM = revenue recognition accuracy. One company, three distinct PM roles that would require different interviews and different skill profiles.
> *PM Lesson: The job title "Product Manager" describes the role, not the specialization. Always ask which archetype a role maps to before accepting.*

## Common Pitfalls
- **Generalist Trap**: Staying a generalist too long in a market that rewards specialists leads to being outcompeted by narrower, deeper candidates at senior levels.
- **Wrong Metric Fluency**: Applying Growth PM metrics (DAU, activation) to a Platform PM role (where success is developer adoption and API reliability) signals a mismatch in mental model.
- **Specialization Lock-in**: Over-indexing on one specialization early (e.g., AI PM at one company) can make it harder to reposition if the domain shifts.

## Specialization Reference

| Archetype | Primary Focus | Defining Hard Skill | Example Companies |
|-----------|---------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Core / General PM | Feature delivery, user value | Prioritization, PRD writing | Most mid-stage startups |
| Technical PM | Developer products, APIs, infra | System design, API patterns | Stripe, Twilio, AWS |
| Growth PM | Acquisition, activation, retention | SQL, A/B testing, funnel analysis | Meta, HubSpot, Duolingo |
| Data PM | Data products, analytics tools | Data modeling, BI tooling | Snowflake, Tableau, dbt |
| AI / ML PM | Model-backed features, AI systems | ML fundamentals, model evaluation | Anthropic, Cohere, Glean |
| Platform PM | Internal platforms, shared services | Infrastructure thinking, developer UX | Uber, Airbnb, Netflix |
| Consumer PM | Mobile/web products for end users | User research, behavioral psychology | Spotify, TikTok, Pinterest |
| Enterprise PM | B2B SaaS, complex buyer journeys | Discovery in multi-stakeholder orgs | Salesforce, Workday, SAP |
