# Enterprise PM

## 📖 Definition
An Enterprise PM (B2B PM) is an archetype that builds products for "Organizations." This role is defined by **Complexity, Multi-stakeholder Decisions, and High Contract Value**. Unlike B2C, where the "User" is the "Buyer," in B2B the "Buyer" (CFO/CTO) is often a different person than the "User" (Employee). Enterprise PMs must solve for **Security, Compliance, Permissions, and Integration** while still trying to provide a good user experience for the employee.

## 🎯 Why It Matters
B2B products are the "Operating Systems" of the global economy.
1. **LTV (Lifetime Value)**: An enterprise customer might pay $1,000,000+ per year and stay for 5+ years.
2. **Predictability**: Revenue is driven by contracts and "Sales Pipelines" rather than "Viral Loops."
3. **Mission Criticality**: If an enterprise product (like Slack or Salesforce) goes down, entire companies stop working.

## 🏗️ How to Apply It
1. **Stakeholder Mapping**: Identify the "Economic Buyer," the "Technical Champion," and the "End User." You must satisfy all three.
2. **The "Sales Assist"**: Work closely with the Sales and Solutions Engineering teams to understand "Deal Blockers" (e.g., "We need SSO to close this $500k deal").
3. **Roadmap Transparency**: Unlike B2C, you must share your "Forward-looking Roadmap" with customers so they can plan their own internal budgets and training.
4. **Compliance & Security**: Relentlessly focus on SOC2, GDPR, HIPAA, and Role-Based Access Control (RBAC). In B2B, these are "Features" that close deals.

## 📦 Real-World Example
> **Salesforce — The "CPQ" PM — 2018**
> The Enterprise PM for "Configure, Price, Quote" (CPQ) at Salesforce doesn't focus on "Engagement." Instead, they focus on **"Quote Accuracy"** and **"Time to Close."** Their product has to handle thousands of "Custom Rules" for different companies. When a major customer like IBM needed to integrate their legacy SAP data into the quote flow, the Enterprise PM had to design a "Connector Framework" that allowed custom data mappings without requiring a custom build for every client.
> *PM Lesson: In Enterprise, 'Boring' features like 'Audit Logs' and 'SSO' are the ones that generate the most revenue.*

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls
- **The "Feature Factory"**: Saying "Yes" to every custom request from a "Big Customer" until the product becomes a bloated, unmaintainable mess.
- **Ignoring the End-User**: Focusing so much on the "Buyer" (who pays the bill) that the product becomes painful for the "User" to use, leading to eventual churn when a better "Bottom-up" competitor arrives.
- **Slow Innovation**: Getting trapped in 12-month release cycles due to "Customer Fear of Change."
