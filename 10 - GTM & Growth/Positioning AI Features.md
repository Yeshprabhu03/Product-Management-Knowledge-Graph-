# 📍 Positioning AI Features

## 📖 Definition
Positioning is the act of designing the product's offering and image to occupy a distinctive place in the mind of the target market. In AI, this involves moving beyond "Powered by AI" (which is a commodity) to "Benefit-focused" positioning. It answers the question: "How does this specific AI make the user's life 10x better than the non-AI alternative?" Positioning can be focused on **Efficiency** (faster), **Quality** (better), or **Capability** (new things you couldn't do before).

## 🎯 Why It Matters for PMs
For an AI Product Manager, positioning is the "Filter" for all product decisions. It matters because:
1. **Determines the Roadmap**: If you are positioned as the "Most Accurate" tool, you must prioritize research and model quality over speed or cost.
2. **Cuts Through the Hype**: Helping users understand your specific niche in a market where every product is claiming to be "the most advanced AI."
3. **Aligns Sales and Marketing**: Providing a clear "story" that the sales team can use to explain why your AI is different from a generic competitor like ChatGPT.

## 🏗️ How to Apply It
1. **Identify the "Job to be Done"**: Don't position the "AI"; position the "Task" it completes for the user (e.g., "The AI that writes your legal briefs").
2. **Define Your "Wedge"**: Find the one narrow domain where your AI is 10x better than a general-purpose model because of your proprietary data or UX.
3. **Choose Your Positioning Axis**: Are you the "Easiest to Use" AI, the "Most Secure" AI, or the "Lowest Cost" AI? Pick one and optimize for it.
4. **Use "Outcome-based" Language**: Replace technical terms (e.g., "Transformer-based summarization") with user benefits (e.g., "Summarize 100 emails in 2 seconds").
5. **Differentiate from "General AI"**: Explain why a user should use your specialized tool instead of just pasting their data into a generic chatbot.

## 📦 Real-World Example
> **Grammarly's "Personal Editor" Positioning — 2023**
> Grammarly didn't position its new Generative AI features as a "Chatbot." They positioned it as an **"AI Writing Partner."** By staying within the "Writing" context, they avoided competing with ChatGPT as a general knowledge tool. They focused their positioning on "Tone," "Conciseness," and "Style," which were the specific needs of their professional user base. This allowed them to maintain their "specialized" value even as general LLMs became more powerful.
> *PM Lesson: The more general the model, the more specific your positioning needs to be to win a market.*

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls
- **Commodity Positioning**: Using generic terms like "AI-powered" or "Smart" that provide no information about the product's actual competitive advantage.
- **Positioning the "Model" Instead of the "Product"**: Bragging about "using GPT-4o" when users don't care about the architecture, only the result.
- **Inconsistent Positioning**: Claiming to be "Privacy-First" in your marketing while using a third-party AI provider that doesn't have a clear data retention policy.
