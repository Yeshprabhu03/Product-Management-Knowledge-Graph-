#Crossing the Chasm

##Definition
A model developed by Geoffrey Moore that describes the gap between the Early Adopters (visionaries who seek a competitive advantage) and the Early Majority (pragmatists who seek proven reliability). For technology products, this "Chasm" is the most dangerous point in the lifecycle.

##Why It Matters for PMs
Many AI products have "impressive demos" that attract visionaries but fail to solve a business-critical problem with enough reliability for the mass market. PMs must recognize when they are at the Chasm and shift their focus from "Innovation" to "Whole Product" (security, support, and proven ROI).

##How to Apply It
1. Identify the "Beachhead": Choose one specific niche market where the pain is highest.
2. Build the "Whole Product": Go beyond the core AI feature to include compliance, integrations, and training.
3. Secure "Pragmatist" References: Focus on getting one high-profile customer in your beachhead to vouch for you.
4. Shift Messaging: Move from "Revolutionary Tech" to "Industry Standard Solution."

##Real-World Example
> **Enterprise AI Startups — 2024**
> Thousands of AI startups are currently stuck in the Early Adopter phase. They have built "Co-pilots" that individuals love, but they haven't "Crossed the Chasm" to the Early Majority (IT Departments and CFOs). The products that cross are those that stop pitching "AI Magic" and start providing SOC2 compliance, data privacy guarantees, and clear case studies showing a 20% reduction in operational cost.
> *PM Lesson: The Chasm is crossed with reliability and evidence, not with more features.*

##Common Pitfalls
- Trying to target the whole market at once before winning a single niche.
- Thinking that because Early Adopters love the product, the rest of the world will too.
- Ignoring the "Operational" requirements (security, billing, SLAs) that pragmatists demand.
