# 🛠️ North Star Framework

## 📖 Definition
The North Star Framework is a model for managing products by identifying a single, crucial metric (the North Star Metric) that best captures the core value that your product delivers to its customers.

## 🎯 When Would I Use This?
1. **Organizational Alignment**: When different teams are pulling in different directions and need a unifying goal.
2. **Success Definition**: When you need to define what "winning" looks like beyond just revenue.
3. **KPI Setting**: To derive team-level metrics from a high-level strategic goal.

## 🏗️ How to Apply It
1. **The North Star Metric (NSM)**: Must be a leading indicator of success, represent value to the customer, and be measurable.
2. **Input Metrics**: Identify 3-5 sub-metrics that drive the NSM (e.g., for Spotify: Time Spent Listening is the NSM; Inputs are Breadth of Discovery, Frequency of Return).
3. **Visualize the Tree**: Map how daily tasks move the input metrics, which in turn move the North Star.

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls
- **Choosing a Vanity Metric**: Using something like "Registered Users" which doesn't reflect actual value delivery.
- **Ignoring Revenue**: The NSM should lead to revenue, but if you ignore the business model entirely, you'll go bankrupt.
