# North Star Framework

## Definition
A model for managing a product by identifying a single, core metric (the North Star Metric) that best expresses the value that the product delivers to its customers. It is supported by a set of "Input Metrics" that teams can directly influence.

## Why It Matters for PMs
It provides a "Single Source of Truth" that aligns the entire organization. It prevents teams from optimizing for "Vanity Metrics" and ensures that every sprint goal contributes to the long-term strategic success of the product.

## How to Apply It
1. Define the North Star: Identify the one metric that correlates with both user value and business revenue.
2. Identify Input Metrics: Break the North Star down into 3-5 levers (e.g., Breadth, Depth, Frequency).
3. Align Teams: Map each product team or squad to a specific input metric.
4. Track and Communicate: Use the North Star as the primary focus of all executive and team reviews.

## Real-World Example
> **Airbnb — 2013**
> Airbnb's North Star is "Nights Booked." Input metrics: number of active listings (supply), search-to-book conversion (demand), and repeat booking rate (retention). Every team maps their sprint goals to one of these inputs. When the photography experiment doubled NYC revenue, it was approved at scale because it demonstrably moved the "active listings" input metric.
> *PM Lesson: The North Star only works if every team can draw a straight line from their work to an input metric.*

## Common Pitfalls
- Choosing a "Revenue" metric as the North Star (revenue is the result of value, not the value itself).
- Having a North Star that is too far removed from the daily work of the teams.
- Not updating the North Star as the product matures or the strategy changes.
