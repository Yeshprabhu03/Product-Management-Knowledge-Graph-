# Amazon Working Backwards

## Definition
A product development methodology where the team starts with the customer and the final vision, and then works backward to the current state. The core artifact is the PR/FAQ (Press Release and Frequently Asked Questions).

## Why It Matters for PMs
It forces extreme clarity on the customer value before any code is written. By writing the "Press Release" first, PMs must define a product that is actually worth talking about, rather than just a collection of features.

## How to Apply It
1. Write the Press Release: A one-page document announcing the product as if it had already launched.
2. Draft the Internal FAQ: Answer the hard questions about cost, safety, and competition.
3. Draft the External FAQ: Answer the questions customers will actually have.
4. Define the Customer Experience: Use wireframes or storyboards to show how the product works.

## Real-World Example
> **Amazon Echo — 2014**
> Before a single engineer was assigned, the Echo team wrote the press release: "Amazon Launches Voice-Controlled Speaker that Answers Questions, Plays Music, and Controls Your Smart Home." The internal FAQ included the hard question: "What if Siri already does this?" The answer forced them to define their moat (always-on, home-native, integrated with Amazon Prime) — shaping the product before any code was written.
> *PM Lesson: If you can't write the press release, you don't understand the product yet. The PR/FAQ is a clarity tool, not a marketing one.*

## Common Pitfalls
- Using the PR/FAQ as a "Sign-off" document instead of a "Discovery" tool.
- Not updating the PR/FAQ as new information is learned during development.
- Writing a "Boring" press release that doesn't actually solve a major user pain.
