# Stakeholder Communication

## Definition
Stakeholder Communication is the systematic process of sharing information, managing expectations, and gathering feedback from the diverse group of people impacted by a product. This includes "Internal" stakeholders (Sales, Marketing, Legal, Support, Executives) and "External" stakeholders (Customers, Partners, Regulators). The goal is to create **Alignment** — ensuring that everyone understands the "What," "Why," and "When" of the product roadmap, even when their individual priorities might conflict.

## Why It Matters for PMs
For a Product Manager, communication is 80% of the job. It matters because:
1. **Builds Buy-in**: Ensuring that other departments (like Sales or Marketing) are excited about and prepared for upcoming launches.
2. **Reduces Friction**: Proactively addressing the concerns of "skeptical" stakeholders (like Legal or Security) before they become "blockers."
3. **Manages the "Say-Do" Gap**: Ensuring that the expectations of the market (set by Sales/Marketing) match the actual reality of what the product can do.

## How to Apply It
1. **Map Your Stakeholders**: Identify who has "High Interest" and "High Power" over your product and tailor your communication frequency to each group.
2. **Choose the Right Medium**: Use "Synchronous" meetings for complex negotiations and "Asynchronous" documents (like a weekly Loom or email) for status updates.
3. **Tailor the Message**: Talk to Sales about "Revenue and Features," to Engineering about "Scope and Complexity," and to Executives about "Strategy and ROI."
4. **Use "No Surprises" Communication**: If a date is going to slip or a feature is being cut, tell the impacted stakeholders as soon as you are 80% sure.
5. **Create a "Feedback Loop"**: Don't just "broadcast" information; create formal channels (like a Slack channel or a monthly sync) for stakeholders to share their ground-level feedback.

## Real-World Example
> **The "Internal Launch" — 2022**
> A PM at a SaaS company was launching a major AI feature. They didn't just tell the Sales team "it's live." They spent two weeks doing an "Internal Tour": 1. Demoing the bot for the Support team to answer their security fears. 2. Providing the Sales team with a "Cheat Sheet" of what the AI *can't* do. 3. Recording a 3-minute video for the Executives on the "Gross Margin" impact. This comprehensive communication meant the launch was smooth and every department was perfectly aligned on day one.
> *PM Lesson: Your product isn't "shipped" until every stakeholder knows how it impacts their world.*

## Common Pitfalls
- **The "Silo" Mentality**: Building in secret and only telling stakeholders about a feature a few days before it launches, which leads to resentment and "last-minute" blocking.
- **Over-promising to Sales**: Telling the sales team "it's coming in Q3" when you haven't even finished the discovery phase, leading to broken promises to customers.
- **Ignoring the "Non-Technical" Stakeholders**: Forgetting to update the Support or Customer Success teams, who are the ones that have to deal with the fallout if the product fails.
